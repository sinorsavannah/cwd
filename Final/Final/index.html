BTW what is a BTU?

BTU stands for British Thermal Unit and it is way to measure heat or energy. One BTU is equivalent to approximately 1055.06 joules or 0.293071 watt hour, which are units you might be more familiar with. One BTU is also the amount of heat you need to warm a pound of water up by 1 degree fahrenheit, if it is easier to think of it that way. A quadrillion BTU, which is our unit of measurement, is 1015 BTU... so a lot.

GW.... George Washington???

Uh no. So there might be some units here you haven't seen before or know the difference between.

Lets starts with GWh vs GW aka giga-watt hours vs giga-watts. So a watt is a unit of power and a giga-watt is one billion watts. What is the difference between a giga-watt and a giga-watt hour? Adding in that time element changes the unit from one of power to one of energy. Energy is a measurement how much power is generated over a given time, so in this instance how many giga-watts over the course of one hour. Giga-watt would be used for capactiy whereas giga-watt hours would be used for usage and generation measurements.

BDT is a bone dry ton. It is equivalent to 2000 pounds of organic materials with zero percent moisture. This would be used to measure biomass like wood.
